Aadhaar Linked Account

This is the basic step. It simply links your Aadhaar number to your bank account for identification and verification purposes.

  • Primarily for KYC (Know Your Customer) and identity verification.
  • Does not automatically guarantee that you will receive scholarship funds.
  • This is only the first step. You need to do more!

DBT Enabled Aadhaar Seeded Account

This is the crucial step for receiving government benefits like scholarships. Your account is specifically "seeded" for Direct Beneficiary Transfers.

  • Your account is designated to receive government funds directly.
  • Crucial for receiving scholarships and other benefits from the government.
  • You must ensure your Aadhaar is "seeded" with a specific bank account for DBT.

Why DBT is So Important

Reduced Delays: Funds are transferred directly from the government to your bank account, cutting out middlemen and reducing delays.

Increased Transparency: You can easily track the status of your scholarship payment, giving you peace of mind.

Common Questions & Tips

Q: Can I have multiple bank accounts linked to my Aadhaar?

Yes, you can. However, only **one** account can be active for DBT at any given time. This is called the 'latest seeded' account and is where your scholarship will be disbursed. Make sure the correct account is seeded for DBT.

Q: What if I have Aadhaar-linked but not DBT-seeded?

Your scholarship payment will **fail**. The Government can only transfer funds to an account that is explicitly seeded for DBT. You must contact your bank and get your account seeded to receive your scholarship.

Tip: What document to carry to the bank?

When you go to the bank for Aadhaar seeding, carry a copy of your Aadhaar card and a signed consent form provided by the bank. Some banks may also require a passbook or a check leaf to verify your account details.

Tip: Use NPCI Mapper

You can also check your Aadhaar seeding status directly on the NPCI website using their Aadhaar Mapper. This is a quick way to confirm which bank account is currently receiving your DBT payments.

What to do next?

Make sure your Aadhaar is not just linked, but **seeded** for DBT. Visit your bank branch or use their official website/app to complete this process.
